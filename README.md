Market Performance, Structural Analysis & Decision Framework (2016–2023)

Analytical Consult – Rock Quant

Overview

This repository presents a structured analytical framework for evaluating fuel price dynamics in Argentina from 2016 to 2023. The work integrates market performance analysis, volatility diagnostics, regional disparity assessment, and a decision-support architecture.

The objective is to transition from static reporting to a continuous pricing intelligence system capable of supporting real-time monitoring, anomaly detection, and data-driven intervention.

Executive Summary

Between 2016 and 2023, Argentina’s fuel market transitioned from a relatively stable, inflation-aligned system into a high-cost, volatile, and structurally fragmented environment.

A structural break emerged in 2022, characterized by:

- Rapid price escalation
- Significant increase in absolute volatility
- Expansion of regional price disparities
- Divergence in company-level pricing strategies

A key analytical finding is the divergence between absolute and relative volatility in 2023:

- Absolute volatility increased significantly
- Relative volatility declined

This reflects a statistical compression effect driven by elevated price levels rather than genuine market stabilization. As a result, relative volatility alone is insufficient for assessing market stability.

Market Evolution (2016–2023)
Phase 1: Controlled Inflation (2016–2019)
- Moderate and predictable price increases
- Low volatility
- Limited regional variation
- Stable competitive equilibrium
Phase 2: Disruption (2020)
- Demand contraction associated with COVID-19
- Continued upward pricing trend
- Moderate increase in volatility
- Influence of supply chain disruptions and policy interventions
Phase 3: Recovery and Coordination (2021)
- Strong upward price trajectory
- Decline in relative volatility
- Evidence of coordinated pricing behavior
Phase 4: Structural Break (2022)
- Absolute volatility increased by 139.9%
- Regional price gap expanded by 147.9%
- Breakdown of pricing coordination
- Driven by global energy shocks and currency instability
Phase 5: Hyper-Expansion and Fragmentation (2023)
- Prices increased by more than 130%
- Continued growth in absolute volatility
- Decline in relative volatility due to scaling effects
- Regional disparities expanded by 148.4%
Core Analytical Insight: Volatility Masking Effect

The observed decline in relative volatility alongside increasing absolute volatility represents a measurement distortion:

- Rising price levels reduce proportional dispersion
- Absolute price movements become larger
- Apparent stability masks underlying instability

Decision-making frameworks must therefore incorporate both:

- Absolute volatility metrics
- Relative volatility measures
- Global Benchmarking Context
- Efficient Market Characteristics
- Strong correlation with global crude oil benchmarks
- Transparent pricing mechanisms
- Volatility driven by supply-demand fundamentals
- Predictable adjustment patterns
- Argentine Market Characteristics
- Price increases during periods of demand contraction
- Weak alignment with global benchmarks
- Significant regional price divergence
- Influence of policy and structural inefficiencies
- Emerging Market Comparison

Argentina exhibits characteristics common in structurally constrained markets:

- Currency instability
- Subsidy regimes
- Supply chain inefficiencies

These conditions contribute to reduced efficiency, increased volatility, and regional inequality.
